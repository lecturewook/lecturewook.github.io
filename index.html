Welcome!
My research examines immigrant representation, societal perceptions of immigrant communities, and cultural assimilation processes. I am particularly interested in the internal dynamics within immigrant communities, native citizens’ perceptions of immigrants, examining the political representation and voting behavior of immigrants and other marginalized groups in response to recent changes in population structure. To address these issues, I draw on interdisciplinary perspectives from political science, demography, and psychology. Additionally, I specialize in experimental political science and mixed-methods approaches, which enable a more comprehensive exploration of my research interests. I hold a Bachelor’s degree in political science and Master’s degree in public policy. If you share similar research interests or would like to discuss related topics, please feel free to reach out.
In addition, I enjoy reading, exercising (especially swimming, soccer, tennis, and fitness), fishing, and participating in volunteer activities. If you share an interest in these hobbies and would like to engage in them together, please feel free to contact me! :)
Thank you!
